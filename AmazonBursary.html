Amazon Bursary

Helping students with financial need — South Africa

About this Bursary

The Amazon Recruitment Bursary was launched in 2018 by Amazon Development Centre for people from previously disadvantaged backgrounds. Successful applicants will receive funding from Amazon to cover tuition fees, accommodation and additional academic costs. Additionally, successful applicants must be committed to completing a paid Amazon internship (in summer vacation) during which applicants are assessed for a full-time permanent Software Development Engineer (SDE) role to start upon graduation. Amazon is a place where motivated employees thrive and where employee ownership and accountability lead to meaningful results.

Who Can Apply - Requirements

    StudyTrust, on behalf of Amazon, invites current Computer Science, Computer Engineering, and Software Engineering undergraduate students who will in 2026 be entering into their final year of a three-year degree OR the third year of a four-year degree, to apply for this bursary. Applicants should intend to continue studying for an Honours degree in 2027 (after completing the internship) and have a keen interest in software development engineering.

    Important: Please ensure you meet the following eligibility criteria before submitting your application.

    Eligibility Criteria:

    • Field of Study: Applicants must be pursuing Undergraduate Degrees in fields related to Computer Science, Computer Engineering, and Software Engineering. Specializations in Machine Learning or Artificial Intelligence are also eligible.
    • Education Completion: Candidates should plan to complete their Tertiary education (up to Honours level or NQF level 8) by the end of 2027.
    • Master’s Level: Master’s level students are not eligible to apply.
    • Availability: Applicants must be available to start full-time permanent employment with Amazon in January 2028, contingent upon receiving a job offer after the internship assessment.
    • Career Plans & Motivation: Applicants must display a keen interest in the field of software development engineering and Amazon. During the internship, they will be assessed for a full-time permanent role with Amazon to start upon completion of their studies.
    • South African Universities Only: The bursary is open to students from South African universities.
    • Academic Achievement: Current students who are consistently obtaining an average of 65% and above.
    • Citizenship: Only applications from South African citizens will be considered.
      Obligations:

    Successful applicants must be committed to completing a paid Amazon internship (during summer vacation), and participate in training opportunities at Amazon.

    To qualify for 100% of the bursary funding, candidates who are awarded must be open to accepting a full-time, permanent position for a duration equal to the years of bursary received, contingent upon receiving a job offer after the internship assessment.

What this Bursary Covers

    The Amazon Recruitment Bursary will cover the costs (capped) of the following:

  • Tuition
  • Residence/private accommodation
  • Subsistence
  • Stationery and books
  • A personal computer
  • Transport
