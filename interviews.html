Before the Interview

Research the Company

Spend at least 2-3 hours researching the company thoroughly. This shows genuine interest and helps you ask intelligent questions.

  • Visit their website and read their mission, values, and recent news
  • Check their social media profiles and recent posts
  • Look up the company on LinkedIn and see who works there
  • Research your interviewer on LinkedIn if possible
  • Understand their products, services, and competitors

Prepare Your Documents

Have all necessary documents ready and organized:

  • Multiple copies of your CV (at least 5)
  • Cover letter tailored to the position
  • Portfolio or work samples (if applicable)
  • References list with contact information
  • Certificates and qualifications
  • ID document or passport

Plan Your Journey

Never be late for an interview! Plan ahead:

  • Know the exact address and how to get there
  • Do a practice run the day before
  • Plan to arrive 10-15 minutes early
  • Have backup transport options
  • Save the interviewer's contact number

Dress for Success

Your appearance makes the first impression:

  • Research the company dress code
  • When in doubt, dress slightly more formal
  • Ensure clothes are clean, ironed, and fit well
  • Keep accessories minimal and professional
  • Good personal hygiene is essential

During the Interview

Make a Great First Impression

The first 30 seconds are crucial:

  • Arrive 10-15 minutes early
  • Greet everyone politely, including reception
  • Firm handshake and eye contact
  • Smile genuinely and be enthusiastic
  • Wait to be invited to sit

Body Language Matters

Non-verbal communication is 55% of your message:

  • Maintain good eye contact (not staring)
  • Sit up straight and lean slightly forward
  • Keep hands visible and use natural gestures
  • Mirror the interviewer's energy level
  • Avoid fidgeting or checking your phone

Use the STAR Method

Structure your answers using STAR for behavioral questions:

  • Situation: Set the context
  • Task: Describe what needed to be done
  • Action: Explain what you did
  • Result: Share the outcome

Ask Smart Questions

Show your interest by asking thoughtful questions:

  • "What does success look like in this role?"
  • "What are the biggest challenges facing the team?"
  • "How would you describe the company culture?"
  • "What opportunities are there for growth?"
  • "What do you enjoy most about working here?"

After the Interview

Send a Thank You Email

Follow up within 24 hours:

  • Thank them for their time
  • Reiterate your interest in the position
  • Mention something specific from the conversation
  • Attach any additional documents if requested
  • Keep it brief and professional

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't let these mistakes cost you the job:

  • Being late or arriving too early (more than 15 minutes)
  • Not researching the company
  • Speaking negatively about previous employers
  • Not having questions prepared
  • Being too casual or overly familiar
  • Lying or exaggerating on your CV

Interview Checklist

  • Research company thoroughly
  • Prepare 5+ copies of CV
  • Plan journey and arrive early
  • Dress professionally
  • Prepare STAR examples
  • Prepare questions to ask
  • Bring portfolio/work samples
  • Practice common questions
  • Get good night's sleep
  • Send thank you email

Common Questions

"Tell me about yourself"

Keep it professional and relevant. Structure: Current situation ‚Üí Relevant experience ‚Üí Why you're interested in this role. Keep it under 2 minutes.

"Why do you want this job?"

Show you've researched the company. Connect your skills and interests to the role. Mention specific aspects that excite you.

"What's your greatest weakness?"

Choose a real weakness that won't disqualify you. Explain how you're actively working to improve it. Show self-awareness.

"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

Show ambition but be realistic. Align your goals with potential growth in the company. Demonstrate long-term thinking.
